Good to know

Common questions.

How long does a custom order take?

Most orders are ready in roughly one to two weeks after you approve the artwork. Timelines shift with the season and order size, so tell us your deadline when you request a quote and we'll let you know what's doable.

Is there a minimum order?

Screen printing is most cost-effective by the dozen, while embroidery and DTF transfers work great for small runs — even a single shirt, hat, or bag. Whatever the size, we'll find the right method.

What artwork do you need?

Vector files (.ai, .eps, .pdf, or .svg) give the cleanest results, but a high-resolution .png or .jpg usually works too. No art ready? Bring us a sketch or an idea and we can help build it.

Are there setup or digitizing fees?

Screen printing includes a one-time screen charge per color, and embroidery includes a one-time digitizing fee to turn your logo into stitches. We spell out any fees up front in your quote — no surprises at pickup.

Can you match our team or school colors?

Yes. We match team and school colors and can work from Pantone (PMS) references to keep your look consistent across uniforms, spirit wear, and signage.

Can you print on items we already own?

Often, yes — just ask first. Some fabrics and finishes take ink or stitching better than others, so we'll check your items before committing to a deadline.

Which method is right for my order?

Big team orders with a few colors lean toward screen printing. Logos on hats, polos, and jackets are perfect for embroidery. Full-color or photo-style art, and smaller quantities, are a great fit for DTF transfers. Not sure? Tell us what you're making and we'll recommend the best fit.
